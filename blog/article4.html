What am I afraid of?

Published on 5/18/23

The definition of fear is "A very unpleasant or disturbing feeling caused by the presence or imminence of danger", and
The definition of phobia is "An anxiety disorder defined by a persistent and excessive fear of an object or situation".

Phobias are often thought of as "irrational" fear compared to other fear people feel, such as trauma. I think all phobias have reasons, and I found 4 ways a phobia can build up inside a person.

  • The first one is when something bad happens in some particular situation causing the situation to become scary.
    For example, an experience of tripping over something in the dark may make the dark freightening.

  • Another is watching somebody else being scared of something.
    For example, if a child who went to a zoo with a parent that was scared of elephants, they might learn that elephants are something to be scared of, and unconsciously grow fear against them.
  • The third one is hearing or reading that something is dangerous.
    Reading an article about how drowning is the 25th most common way to die globally could make someone scared of bodies of water.
  • Lastly, there is fear that is inherited through genes. Though not much about the system is understood, studies such as this one show that passing on fear down generations is something that actually occurs in animals.

My greatest fear is standing in the batter's box, although I literally am in the baseball club and play in games. I was in 4th grade when I was hit in the head during a game, and though I was totally fine because I was wearing a fat helmet, the feeling of a baseball coming straight at my head was very scary, and to this day baseballs that come even a little bit close to my body during my at-bat make me freeze and panic mildly.

This fear has made me do dumb things in games, such as when a pitcher threw 3 balls consecutively near my head and then two strikes right down the middle which I couldn't swing at. I felt the urge to swing at the next ball, which was significantly out of the strike zone, and I ended up striking out from a 3-0 count.
Fear has also caused me to not like batting compared to other things as well. I instead practice a lot of pitching. However, the sport is fun, and I like playing it.

I do feel that I don't want fear to get in the way for me to challenge new things or continue to do things I enjoy.
