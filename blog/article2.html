Why do people lie?

Published on 4/30/23

It is said that people get the ability to and start to lie at around 4 years old and get better at it as we grow. Lying is a process that consists of complex and logical thinking.
In order to lie, it is necessary to think up alternate episodes and explanations for a particular situation. We always lie for somebody, whether it be somebody else or ourselves.
There are many cases when we lie for our own benefit.

Some examples are:

  • Evading punishments
  • stretching truths to become socially accepted in a group of people
  • hiding information to keep from being picked on about it
  • ironically, to gain trust, by lying about having experience or accomplishments

We also lie for other people's benefit.
Some examples of it are:

  • Sparing a friend's feelings by not telling a friend about how bad their food actually is
  • Not reporting a friend to the police for illegal activities

Lying sounds like a very bad thing to do. However, in reality, many lies are used to protect people, whether that be someone else or themselves. According to a study done in Canada in 2018, the most common lie was a white lie.
The answer to "why do we lie?" might be "because we are nice to people".
